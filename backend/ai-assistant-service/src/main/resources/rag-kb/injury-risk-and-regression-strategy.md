# Injury Risk Control and Regression Strategy

This project uses conservative coaching rules when users mention pain, prior injury, or recent setback.

Risk-control rules:

1. Pain during a movement is a decision signal, not something to ignore.
2. Regress range, load, speed, or complexity before removing all training.
3. Keep symptom-free alternatives active to preserve confidence and capacity.

Regression ladder:

- Level 1: reduce load and tempo speed.
- Level 2: reduce range of motion or simplify stance/grip.
- Level 3: switch to adjacent pattern (for example bilateral to supported unilateral).
- Level 4: replace provocative movement temporarily and rebuild gradually.

Session structure under risk context:

- Longer warmup and joint preparation.
- Lower peak intensity and fewer near-failure sets.
- More control-oriented accessories and anti-rotation stability work.
- Objective stop criteria for pain increase.

Return-to-load guidance:

- Increase only one stress variable at a time.
- Keep at least 24-48h between repeated high-stress loading on the same region.
