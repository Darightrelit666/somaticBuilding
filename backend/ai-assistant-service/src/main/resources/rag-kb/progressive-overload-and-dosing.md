# Progressive Overload and Training Dose

Core principles used by coaches in this project:

1. Progressive overload should be gradual and measurable.
2. Increase one variable at a time when possible: load, reps, sets, density, or complexity.
3. Keep at least 1-3 reps in reserve (RIR) for most main work in non-peak phases.
4. Hard effort without movement quality is not valid progression.
5. Weekly volume should match recovery capacity, not only motivation.

Practical dosing guide:

- Beginner: 2-4 sessions/week, 45-70 min/session, emphasize movement quality and repeatability.
- Intermediate: 3-5 sessions/week, 60-90 min/session, use structured load progression.
- Advanced: 4-6 sessions/week, periodize intensity and deload windows.

Load progression checkpoints:

- Add load only if technique and tempo remain stable.
- Use small increments (2-5%) before adding large jumps.
- If sleep, soreness, or pain markers degrade, reduce volume before reducing technical work.

Deload guidance:

- Typical deload every 4-8 weeks depending on fatigue accumulation.
- Reduce volume by ~30-50% while keeping movement patterns.
