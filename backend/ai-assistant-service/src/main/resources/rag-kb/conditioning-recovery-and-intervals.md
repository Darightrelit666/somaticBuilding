# Conditioning, Recovery, and Interval Design

Energy system framing:

- Alactic focus: short explosive efforts (roughly up to 10 seconds), full recovery emphasis.
- Glycolytic focus: moderate duration hard sets (roughly 20-90 seconds), partial recovery.
- Aerobic focus: sustainable efforts and repeatability, supports recovery between hard sets.

Interval programming examples:

- Power emphasis: 6-10 sets, 6-12 seconds work, 60-120 seconds rest.
- Mixed conditioning: 6-12 rounds, 30-60 seconds work, 30-90 seconds rest.
- Aerobic intervals: 4-8 rounds, 2-4 minutes work, 1-2 minutes easy recovery.

Recovery markers that should affect training decisions:

1. Sleep quantity and quality.
2. Resting fatigue and mood state.
3. Joint irritation and persistent stiffness.
4. Performance trend over multiple sessions, not one bad day.

When recovery is insufficient:

- First lower volume and density.
- Keep foundational movement patterns and technical quality.
- Rebuild intensity after readiness markers recover.
